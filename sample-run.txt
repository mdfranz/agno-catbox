The local network subnet appears to be `192.168.2.0/24`, with four primary "top talker" internal hosts generating the majority of the traffic.

### 🚨 Key Threat 1: Active C2 Beaconing
**Host:** `192.168.2.177`
There is clear evidence of Command and Control (C2) beaconing originating from this host.
*   **Indicator of Compromise (IoC):** The host queried and established **116 TLS connections** to the SNI `last8july.dcc-mdr-dev.us`. 
*   **External Destinations:** The traffic resolves to AWS-hosted IP addresses `54.237.28.39` and `50.16.241.120`.
*   **Behavioral Pattern:** The TLS connections occur at a highly frequent and automated interval (averaging ~6 seconds between connections, with many occurring back-to-back or within 10 seconds). 
*   **Data Volume:** The traffic flow is balanced (~180KB uploaded to the server, ~300KB downloaded), which is indicative of an active interactive session or heavy beaconing rather than bulk data exfiltration.

---

### ⚠️ Key Threat 2: P2P Activity & SSDP Amplification Scans
**Host:** `192.168.2.201`
This host is generating substantial noise and triggering nearly all the IDS alerts in the network.
*   **Alerts Triggered:**
    *   `ET INFO Session Traversal Utilities for NAT (STUN Binding Request/Response)` (190 alerts)
    *   `ET DOS Possible SSDP Amplification Scan in Progress` (6 alerts)
*   **Behavioral Pattern:** The host is making a large volume of small connections to dozens of random external IPs worldwide. This pattern typically indicates Peer-to-Peer (P2P) software (like BitTorrent or a decentralized node system), a STUN-based VPN/proxy, or potentially participation in a distributed amplification scan.

---

### 🔍 Suspicious DNS Anomalies
Two internal hosts are making an unusual volume of DNS queries for specific telecommunications domains, with very few actual network connections to back them up:
*   `192.168.2.177` made **100 DNS queries** for `verizon.net` but only completed 2 network flows to the resolved IPs.
*   `192.168.2.201` made **114 DNS queries** for `tmobile.com` but only completed 2 network flows to the resolved IPs.
*   *Note:* This might be benign carrier connectivity checking by mobile devices/hotspots on the local Wi-Fi, but high DNS query counts paired with low connection rates can also be an indicator of DNS tunneling.

---

### 🟢 Benign Internal Traffic (VXLAN)
**Hosts:** `192.168.2.132` and `192.168.2.73`
*   These two IPs are the highest volume talkers in the dataset (over 1,000 flows combined). 
*   However, almost all their traffic is internal UDP over **Port 8472**. This is standard for `VXLAN` overlay networks, indicating these hosts are likely running a local containerized cluster (such as Kubernetes using Flannel).
